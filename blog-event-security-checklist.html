The Complete Event Security Checklist for Organisers in Odisha

A comprehensive security planning guide to ensure safety for guests, staff and property at weddings, corporate events and public gatherings across Odisha.

February 15, 2026 Event Security Guide 10 min read

Organising an event in Odisha — whether it is a grand wedding in Bhadrak, a corporate conference in Bhubaneswar, a religious festival in Cuttack, or a private party in Balasore — requires careful security planning to protect your guests, staff, property and reputation. Without proper security measures, even the best-planned event can turn into a disaster due to gate-crashing, theft, crowd mismanagement or emergency situations.

This comprehensive event security checklist is designed to help event organisers in Odisha plan and execute foolproof security arrangements. Whether you are hiring professional bouncers or security guards, this checklist ensures nothing is overlooked.

Phase 1: Pre-Event Security Planning (2–4 Weeks Before)

The foundation of successful event security begins weeks before the actual event. Proper planning ensures that security personnel, equipment and procedures are all in place well in advance.

1.1 Risk Assessment

  • Identify the type of event (wedding, corporate, public, private party, concert, religious gathering)
  • Estimate the expected number of guests and attendees
  • Assess potential risks: gate-crashing, theft, alcohol-related incidents, VIP threats, fire hazards
  • Evaluate the venue layout for security vulnerabilities (multiple entry points, blind spots, parking areas)
  • Check weather conditions and plan for outdoor event contingencies
  • Identify nearest hospital, fire station and police station

1.2 Security Personnel Planning

  • Determine the number of security guards and bouncers required based on guest count (recommended: 1 guard per 50–75 guests)
  • Decide whether armed security (gunman services) is needed for high-value events
  • Assign bodyguard services for VIP guests if applicable
  • Hire from a PSARA-licensed agency like Jyotiksha Security Agency for trained and verified personnel
  • Brief the security team on venue layout, event schedule and special requirements
  • Assign shift schedules and break rotations for multi-day events

1.3 Venue Security Setup

  • Define and secure all entry/exit points — limit uncontrolled access
  • Set up a registration or invitation verification desk at the main entrance
  • Designate VIP areas with separate access controls
  • Plan parking area security and vehicle access management
  • Identify emergency evacuation routes and mark them clearly with signage
  • Ensure fire extinguishers and first aid kits are available and accessible
  • Test all CCTV cameras and lighting systems at the venue

Phase 2: During the Event — Active Security Management

Once the event begins, your security team must be alert, visible and coordinated. This phase requires constant vigilance and effective communication.

2.1 Entry and Access Control

  • Verify invitations, tickets or wristbands at all entry points
  • Conduct bag checks and pat-downs if appropriate (especially for public events)
  • Monitor for gate-crashers and unauthorised entry attempts
  • Manage the flow of guests to prevent overcrowding at entrances
  • Maintain a log of all visitors, vendors and staff entering the venue

2.2 Crowd Management

  • Position bouncers at strategic locations to prevent crowding and maintain order
  • Monitor alcohol service areas for disorderly behaviour
  • Handle disputes and confrontations through de-escalation techniques
  • Keep emergency exits clear at all times
  • Maintain radio or phone communication between all security personnel

2.3 VIP and Asset Protection

  • Provide close protection for VIP guests and dignitaries
  • Secure gift collection areas and cash counters (especially at weddings)
  • Monitor sound equipment, stage area and backstage access
  • Guard catering and kitchen areas from unauthorised access
  • Patrol parking areas to prevent vehicle theft or damage

2.4 Emergency Preparedness

  • Have a designated security command post or control room
  • Keep first aid personnel on standby
  • Know the location of all fire extinguishers and emergency exits
  • Have emergency contact numbers readily available (police, ambulance, fire)
  • Prepare an evacuation plan and brief key staff

Phase 3: Post-Event Security Procedures

Security responsibilities do not end when the event concludes. Post-event procedures are essential for safety and accountability.

  • Ensure safe dispersal of guests — manage traffic and pedestrians
  • Guard the venue during tear-down and equipment removal
  • Conduct a thorough sweep of the venue for left-behind items or suspicious objects
  • Secure all exits until the last vendor and staff member has departed
  • Submit incident reports and security logs to the event organiser
  • Debrief the security team and document lessons learned

How Many Security Personnel Do You Need?

The number of security guards and bouncers required depends on the size and type of event. Here is a general guideline:

Event Size Guest Count Recommended Security
Small Up to 100 guests 2–3 security guards
Medium 100–300 guests 4–6 guards + 2 bouncers
Large 300–500 guests 8–12 guards + 4 bouncers
Very Large 500+ guests Custom security plan required

For events with VIP guests, high-value gifts or cash, or alcohol service, we recommend additional security personnel and potentially bodyguard services or armed security.
